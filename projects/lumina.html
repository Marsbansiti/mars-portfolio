Lumina

Lumina occupies a converted warehouse in Copenhagen's Refshaleøen district, where industrial heritage meets serene domesticity. The design celebrates the building's raw concrete skeleton while introducing warm oak, brushed limestone, and soft wool textiles to create a tactile counterpoint. Natural light from expansive north-facing windows washes across surfaces throughout the day, revealing subtle material grain and shadow play. The clients — a ceramicist and a photographer — sought a home that doubles as a living gallery for their evolving collection of Nordic craft. Spatial flow is choreographed around a central kitchen island of honed marble, which anchors the open-plan living area. Every element is designed for slowness: deep window seats for reading, a sculptural sink carved from a single stone block, and bedroom alcoves wrapped in acoustic paneling. The result is a sanctuary where craftsmanship, light, and material honesty converge.
