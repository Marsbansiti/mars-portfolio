V

V House is conceived as a refined urban sanctuary that balances neoclassical symmetry with contemporary Asian sensibility. The spatial narrative is anchored by axial planning — arched thresholds, centered compositions, and layered perspectives — creating a sense of procession from public to private zones. The integration of a dedicated spiritual altar space introduces cultural grounding, while soft neutral tones and controlled daylight establish a calm, almost gallery-like atmosphere. The design avoids ornament excess, instead using proportion, rhythm, and subtle detailing to express elegance.

The completed V House achieves a cohesive, high-end residential experience within a narrow townhouse typology. Despite the 5×22m footprint, the design maximizes perceived space through visual continuity, vertical emphasis, and strategic lighting. Each floor transitions seamlessly in function while maintaining a unified design language. The result is a home that feels both timeless and contemporary — delivering comfort, cultural identity, and spatial efficiency — positioning it as a strong benchmark for modern neoclassical living in Ho Chi Minh City.
