Silva

Silva occupies the upper floor of a modernist building overlooking Barcelona’s Eixample district. The design strips away ornament to reveal a dialogue between raw materiality and controlled light. Travertine planes, smoked oak millwork, and linen-wrapped volumes orchestrate a sequence of compression and release throughout the 2,583-square-foot dwelling. The clients — a gallerist and a composer — requested a space that functions as both a sanctuary and a venue for intimate cultural gatherings. Every surface is considered: walls become acoustic baffles, niches frame curated artifacts, and the horizon of the city appears as a living canvas through floor-to-ceiling glazing. The result is an environment where sound, texture, and vista coalesce into a singular, meditative atmosphere.
